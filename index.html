Introduction

Problem

The adoption of online learning by academic institutions has become common. However, this brings along issues such as the disconnect between the students and the instructor, and high drop out rates, among others. Our research looks at the Critical Success Factors (CSFs), which help alleviate these issues.

Solution

The research is broken into two separate sub-projects, each aimed at addressing a specific CSF. One sub-project will focus on Student-Instructor Interactions (SIIs), and the ways in which they can be improved in an asynchronous learning environment. The other will focus on the Gamification CSF which when implemented, could help improve first year Computer Science Courses (CS1).

Student-Instructor Interactions

It is difficult to foster student-instructor interactions in large online classes, and students generally perform poorly in the absence of interactions with the instructor. Online forums often suffer from delayed responses, and sometimes no responses at all. This impacts learning outcomes negatively. Asynchronous lecture videos, although central to online learning, provide students with no efficient ways of asking questions in the middle of a lecture video, and questions verbally posed by instructors to students, do not guarantee responses from the students. With most academic institutions adopting an online learning strategy, effective methods of encouraging student-instructor interactions become key.

We proposed to build a web application that would couple each lecture video with its own dedicated forum, in order to reduce the complexities associated with the current method of making forum posts on the Vula LMS. The web system will also house a mandatory in-lecture quiz system, so that instructors can ask questions and get responses from all students, in an asynchronous environment. Our research questions were as follows:

1. What effect does the pairing of lecture videos with distinct forums, have on Student-Instructor Interactions? Does the close proximity and ease of access compel students to make posts?

2. What is the effect of incorporating mandatory quizzes on Student-Instructor Interactions? Would students like it, and would they feel as if it improves their interactions with the instructor?
